Tensions detected

Mechanical rules from brand-review-template.md § Detectors. Each tension is a flag, not a critique — it's information for /stardust:direct to consider.

T-color-imbalance-primary
Primary blue used only as background, never as text or border
Adobe blue #3b63fb appears 91 times in the captured data, all in usedAs: ["background"]. No instances as text or border. This is intentional brand discipline (one saturated CTA color reserved to filled buttons) but it's worth flagging for direct so variants don't accidentally introduce blue text or blue borders.
T-scale-ad-hoc
Type scale is ad-hoc, not canonical-modular
Heading-size ratios across captured pages: 1.286, 1.0, 1.273, 1.1 — no match for major-third (1.25), perfect-fourth (1.333), or any canonical scale. Adobe uses fluid clamp() sizing in the design samples and the cross-page weighted mode aggregates to mixed discrete sizes. Direction phase should decide whether to (a) adopt a strict modular scale, (b) keep the fluid clamp pattern, or (c) accept the ad-hoc reality and document.
T-mode-A-inversion-needed
Two stardust hard-rules need brand-faithful inversions
(1) Pure black/white surfaces: adobe.com uses literal #000 and #ffffff on every captured page (footer black, body white). The stardust default "no pure black/white" rule must be inverted. (2) Backdrop-blur glassmorphism on nav: the captured nav uses backdrop-filter: blur(64–128px) over rgba(255,255,255,0.64). Brand-faithful and load-bearing.
T-cta-vocab-fragmentation
High distinct-CTA vocabulary across the marketing surface
39 CTAs on home alone, with verbs spanning "View plans and pricing", "Get started", "Free trial", "Buy now", "Learn more", "Sign In", "Explore", "Get help". Some overlap with audience-routing labels ("Creativity & Design", "PDF & E-signatures") that appear as both nav items and CTA candidates in the heuristic. Direction phase should consolidate to a tighter verb set per audience track.
T-geo-redirect-modal
Geolocation redirect modal interrupts above-fold experience
Home screenshot captured with an Italian geolocation modal overlaying the hero ("Questo sito Adobe non corrisponde alla tua posizione geografica"). This is location-dependent and not visible in the U.S. The DOM was captured underneath, but the visible-first-paint experience for international visitors includes an interrupting dialog. Direction phase may choose to redesign or remove.
